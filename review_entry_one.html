The Last Heretic

14 March 2016

At book number fourteen of the Horus Heresy series, I would say this is up there as one of my favorite books, if not the favorite. This is hardly faint praise, as in my opinion there hasn’t been a book that I do not like in the series, even the much-derided Battle for the Abyss.

The First Heretic has also had its fair share of shade thrown at it by a popular YouTube reviewer by the name of Arch Warhammer, and I find the vast majority of his criticisms to be not only pedantic but a demonstration that he did not understand the plot or the lore of the Word Bearers traitor legion.

The main character that we follow around for the majority of the story is a space marine of the legion’s Serrated Suns chapter, and rather than being the carbon copy of Garviel Loken as I expected, Argel Tal is not only one of the most unique protagonists so far, but arguably an even more compelling one than his counterpart in the Sons of Horus.
